# Speaker notes — All-Hands · July 16, 2026

## Slides 1–2 · Intro (the backstory)

Before I show you what's new, let me start with something familiar.

Every all-hands, we get the same reminder from Kyon: *don't forget your daily snapshot.* And he's right — we're a work-from-home company. The snapshot is how management and our own teammates know what we're working on, what's moving, and whether someone's blocked or having problems. Without it, we're all working in the dark.

But here's our honest problem as the ones filling it out: by the end of the day, we've forgotten half of what we did. The day was long, we had a lot to do, and now it's 6 PM and we're staring at a blank form trying to reconstruct our own day. It's not that we don't want to submit — it's that remembering is the hard part.

That problem is the centerpiece of today's update. The tech team went and fixed it — plus a few more things across YoWork and Geopher. Here's what we're covering *(agenda slide)*: YoWork on your phone, the Daily Snapshot moving into YoWork, and scheduling settings so it writes itself — then for Geopher: competitor alerts, a weekly digest, and Xendit going live.

## Slide 4 · Mobile Responsiveness

First: YoWork now works on your phone. Same URL, nothing to install — open it in your browser and the screens actually fit. Check tasks, approve, even sign documents — if an e-signature request comes in while you're out, you can handle it right from your phone.

## Slide 5 · Daily Snapshot (the payoff)

And now, the one I promised at the start. The Daily Snapshot now lives inside YoWork — and it remembers your day for you. It gathers your Lark chats and updates, the meetings on your calendar, and everything you did in YoWork, and drafts the snapshot from what you *actually* did. No more separate Lark tables per team, no more end-of-day amnesia — and everything lands in one place, so leads can review the whole team's day at a glance.

## Slide 6 · Daily Snapshot Settings

You don't even have to remember to write it. Under Settings → Scheduling there's a "Generate Daily Snapshot" toggle — pick the time, timezone, and how you're notified: in-app, Lark, or both. Or hit Run Now. When it runs you get a nudge — "Your daily snapshot is ready" — open it, check the draft, fix anything, approve. Done. The habit Kyon keeps asking for, minus the memory work.

## Slides 7–8 · Geopher: Competitor Overtake Alerts

Switching to Geopher — the theme here is Geopher coming to you instead of you checking the dashboard. If a competitor overtakes your brand — say Brand B passes you in share of voice — Geopher notifies you the moment it happens, showing who passed you and where. You find out the day it happens, not weeks later in a report.

## Slide 9 · Weekly AI-Visibility Digest

Second, a weekly digest of your AI-visibility performance: visibility score, mentions, rank, and what moved against competitors — delivered every week. Skim it Monday morning, and only open the dashboard when something changed.

## Slide 10 · Xendit Live

And last — the Xendit integration we demoed in sandbox last update is now live on production. Real customers can purchase, upgrade, downgrade, and manage plans — Geopher now takes revenue end to end without a manual step.
